# Hope - Landing USP Sections

## Vision of Success

### Smooth Staggered Animations on Section Enter
- When the user scrolls into a USP section, elements appear in a choreographed sequence: background visual fades in, headline slides up, body text follows with a slight delay, and the supporting image or illustration enters last. Each step takes 300-500ms with eased timing, creating a sense of narrative unfolding.
- The animations feel intentional, not decorative. They guide the eye from the most important element (the headline) to the supporting detail (the image), reinforcing the storytelling structure.

### Compelling German Copy
- The three sections read like a short, poetic narrative:
  - Section 1: The student, the canvas, the portrait -- establishing the artistic origin.
  - Section 2: The camera, the fragments, the obsession with detail -- the process of decomposition.
  - Section 3: The 24,236 tiles, the digital mosaic, the invitation to participate -- the culmination available to everyone.
- The German is crisp, uses active voice, and avoids marketing cliches. Short sentences with rhythmic variation. The tone is warm but confident.

### Clean Typography
- Oswald (already loaded) for headlines provides strong vertical rhythm and presence. Body text in a complementary sans-serif maintains readability. Generous line-height (1.5-1.6 for body) and considered letter-spacing for headings.
- Text sizes are fluid (clamp-based) so the narrative feels equally impactful on mobile and desktop without breakpoint jumps.

### Cohesive Visual Identity
- The USP sections feel like they belong to the same page as the particle backdrop and the rest of the landing experience. The color palette, spacing, and motion language are consistent. Nothing feels bolted on.
